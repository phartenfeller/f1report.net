Sergio Mantovani was an Italian privateer who competed in Grand Prix racing during the 1950s, primarily with Maserati machinery. Known for his resilience, he started {{driver_races}} World Championship races, achieving his best result with a fifth-place finish at his home Grand Prix in 1954, earning him two championship points. Though his career was brief and without a podium, Mantovani embodied the spirit of the era's independent racers.
