Gilles Villeneuve, one of Formula 1's most revered figures, epitomized raw speed and audacious car control. His brief but spectacular career showcased an aggressive, flamboyant driving style, often pushing his Ferrari to – and beyond – its limits, creating iconic moments of sheer automotive ballet. Though he competed in just {{driver_races}} races, his legend grew through thrilling battles and a fearless approach that captivated fans worldwide. His tragic passing at Zolder in 1982 cemented his status as a heroic, almost mythical, figure in motorsport history.
