Troy Ruttman holds a unique place in Formula 1 history as the youngest winner of the Indianapolis 500, a race that counted towards the World Championship from 1950 to 1960. His victory in 1952 at just 22 years and 80 days old showcased remarkable talent and composure for such a demanding event. While his Grand Prix career outside of the Indy 500 was limited, his achievement at the Brickyard cemented his name in the sport's record books for decades.
