Born in Germany, Claes endured an exceptionally brief but impactful Formula 1 career. He participated in just one Grand Prix, the 1952 German Grand Prix, driving a privateer AFM. Although he did not finish the race, his entry marked him as one of the earliest German drivers to compete in the post-war Formula 1 World Championship. His appearance underscores the diverse field of privateers and local heroes who populated the grids in the sport's formative years.
