Alfonso de Portago, a Spanish aristocrat and true Renaissance man, brought a flamboyant and adventurous spirit to Formula 1 in the mid-1950s. Known for his diverse talents spanning bobsleigh, horse racing, and even aviation, de Portago competed in {{driver_races}} Grand Prix, securing {{driver_podiums}} podium finish. His brief but impactful F1 career, primarily with Ferrari, was marked by raw speed and a daring approach, embodying the gentleman racer era before his tragic death during the 1957 Mille Miglia.
