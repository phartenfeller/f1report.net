Felipe Nasr entered Formula 1 in 2015 with Sauber, bringing significant backing and a strong junior career as the 2009 Formula BMW Europe champion and 2011 British Formula 3 champion. He notably scored points on his debut at the Australian Grand Prix, finishing fifth. Over two seasons, he participated in {{driver_races}} Grands Prix, often outperforming the car's capabilities. His final F1 point came at the chaotic 2016 Brazilian Grand Prix, securing Sauber a crucial tenth place in the Constructors' Championship.
