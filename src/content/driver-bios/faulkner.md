Faulkner, a name not commonly associated with the modern Formula 1 era, competed in an earlier, more perilous period of motorsport. His single Grand Prix start came at the 1950 Indianapolis 500, an event that, for several years, counted as part of the nascent Formula 1 World Championship. Though his F1 career was brief, his participation links him to the very origins of the sport's highest echelon, representing one of the {{driver_races}} drivers to have competed in a World Championship event. His primary racing success and legacy were built within American open-wheel racing, not Formula 1 itself.
