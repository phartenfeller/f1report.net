Luca Badoer is a unique figure in Formula 1 history, primarily known for his extensive tenure as a test driver for Ferrari during their dominant early 2000s era. Despite competing in {{driver_races}} Grands Prix for Scuderia Italia, Minardi, and Forti, he holds the unenviable record for the most race starts without scoring a point. His long-awaited return to race duties with Ferrari in 2009, substituting for the injured Felipe Massa, was a poignant moment, though ultimately challenging. Badoer's dedication and integral role behind the scenes for one of F1's most successful teams cemented his place as a respected, if statistically unheralded, part of the paddock.
