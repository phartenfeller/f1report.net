Heikki Kovalainen, a Finnish talent, entered Formula 1 with a reputation for speed honed in GP2. His F1 career, spanning {{driver_races}} races, saw him achieve his sole victory at the 2008 Hungarian Grand Prix, a highlight during his time with McLaren. While his raw pace was evident, particularly in qualifying, consistency sometimes eluded him. Later in his career, he became a key figure in the nascent Lotus Racing team (later Caterham), providing valuable experience and helping to establish the new outfit in the sport. Though his F1 tenure was relatively short, Kovalainen demonstrated flashes of brilliance and contributed to the development of emerging teams.
