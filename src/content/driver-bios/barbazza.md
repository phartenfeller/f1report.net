Fabrizio Barbazza was an Italian driver who competed in two separate stints in Formula 1, first with AGS in 1991 and then with Minardi in 1993. Known for his distinctive helmet design and often battling at the back of the grid, he managed to score points in 1993. His career was unfortunately cut short by a severe IndyCar crash in 1994, from which he made a remarkable recovery. Barbazza started {{driver_races}} Grands Prix, with a best finish of 6th.
