Brian Henton, often affectionately known as "Hall" due to his distinctive helmet design, was a British driver whose career spanned parts of eight seasons from 1975 to 1982. A two-time Formula Two European Champion, Henton struggled to translate that success to Formula 1, often competing with less competitive machinery. Despite his talent, he never scored a point in {{driver_races}} starts, making him one of the most experienced drivers to not achieve a top-six finish under the traditional points system. His persistence and journey through various privateer teams, however, made him a recognizable figure of his era.
