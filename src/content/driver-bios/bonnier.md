Joakim Bonnier was a Swedish nobleman and versatile racer whose career spanned from sports cars to Formula 1. He achieved a significant milestone by winning the 1959 Dutch Grand Prix, marking BRM's first-ever Formula 1 victory. Known for his gentlemanly demeanor off-track and fierce competitive spirit on it, Bonnier competed in {{driver_races}} Grands Prix, securing {{driver_podiums}} podium finishes and {{driver_poles}} pole positions. Beyond his driving, he was instrumental in establishing the Grand Prix Drivers' Association (GPDA), advocating for improved safety standards in an era of perilous racing.
