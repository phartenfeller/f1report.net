Jack Fairman was a true privateer in the early years of Formula 1, renowned for his perseverance and willingness to compete with often outdated machinery. His career spanned from 1950 to 1961, participating in {{driver_races}} World Championship Grands Prix, primarily with his own cars or small British teams. Fairman famously started the 1959 British Grand Prix in a Cooper T51, before handing it over to Stirling Moss during the race – a practice allowed at the time – who then drove it to a second-place finish. While he never achieved a podium finish himself, his dedication embodied the spirit of independent racers in the sport's nascent era.
