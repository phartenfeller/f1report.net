Ricardo Rosset was a Brazilian driver who competed in Formula 1 across four seasons in the late 1990s. Known for his consistent funding and respectable, if not spectacular, pace in junior categories, he debuted with Footwork in 1996. Rosset moved to the new Lola MasterCard team in 1997, a project that famously collapsed after a single, unclassified race entry. His final season came in 1998 with Tyrrell, where he often struggled to match his teammate's performance, bringing his career to a close after {{driver_races}} starts.
