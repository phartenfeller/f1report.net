The Rodríguez brothers, Ricardo and Pedro, were Mexican racing icons whose lives were tragically cut short. Ricardo, the younger sibling, made his F1 debut at just 19, instantly impressing with his speed and becoming the youngest driver at the time to score championship points. Known for his aggressive, no-fear driving style, he was considered a future world champion before his fatal accident at a non-championship race in Mexico in 1962. Pedro continued his brother's legacy, becoming a versatile and successful sports car racer while also competing in Formula 1, securing {{driver_wins}} victories before his own death in a sports car event in Germany in 1971.
