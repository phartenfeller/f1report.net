Charles de Tornaco, known by his racing pseudonym "Frère", was a Belgian privateer who competed in the early years of the Formula 1 World Championship. Driving predominantly for his own Ecurie Nationale Belge team, he entered a handful of races in the early 1950s. While he did not score championship points, his participation as a passionate private entrant embodied the spirit of the nascent era of Grand Prix racing, where many independent drivers battled against the emerging factory teams.
