Bob Bondurant, an American racer, briefly competed in Formula 1 during the mid-1960s, participating in {{driver_races}} Grand Prix races. Primarily known for his success in sports car racing, including a class win at Le Mans, Bondurant's F1 career saw him race for Ferrari and BRM. His best result was a fourth-place finish at the 1966 Monaco Grand Prix. After a significant racing accident in 1967, he became a renowned high-performance driving instructor, founding the Bob Bondurant School of High Performance Driving.
