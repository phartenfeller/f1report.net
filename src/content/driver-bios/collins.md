Peter Collins was a charismatic and highly regarded British racing driver whose career was tragically cut short. Known for his natural talent and smooth driving style, he quickly rose through the ranks, becoming a key figure in the dominant Ferrari team of the mid-1950s. Collins famously handed over his car to Juan Manuel Fangio during the 1956 Italian Grand Prix, a selfless act that helped Fangio secure the championship.
