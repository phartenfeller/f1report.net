Philippe Alliot was a French driver known for his flamboyant style and enduring presence in Formula 1 during the 1980s and early 1990s. Competing for teams like RAM, Ligier, Larrousse, and McLaren, he amassed {{driver_races}} starts, showcasing remarkable longevity even without reaching the very top of the sport. While he never secured a podium finish, his spirited driving and occasional flashes of speed made him a recognizable figure in the midfield, often pushing the limits of his machinery. Alliot later transitioned to endurance racing, including multiple Le Mans 24 Hours appearances.
