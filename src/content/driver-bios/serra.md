Chico Serra was a Brazilian driver who competed in Formula 1 during the early 1980s. Known for his spirited, albeit often challenging, outings, he made his debut in 1981 with the Fittipaldi team. Despite the uncompetitive machinery he often found himself in, Serra gained recognition for a notable pitlane altercation with Eliseo Salazar in 1982. He participated in {{driver_races}} Grands Prix, securing a best finish of sixth place, which earned him his sole championship point.
