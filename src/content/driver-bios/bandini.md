Lorenzo Bandini was a talented and versatile Italian driver whose career was tragically cut short. Joining Ferrari in 1964, he quickly became a key figure, known for his speed and adaptability across different car types. Bandini achieved {{driver_wins}} Grand Prix wins and {{driver_podiums}} podiums, often playing a crucial supporting role for John Surtees and later Mike Parkes. His promising career ended during the 1967 Monaco Grand Prix, a poignant loss for both Ferrari and Formula 1.
