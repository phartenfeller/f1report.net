Jean-Christophe Boullion, often referred to as "Jules" by the F1 paddock, had a brief but memorable stint in Formula 1. A product of the Renault development programme, he debuted with Sauber in 1995, brought in to replace Karl Wendlinger. Known for his methodical approach rather than outright speed, Boullion competed in {{driver_races}} Grands Prix, with his best finish being a fifth place at the German Grand Prix. Despite not securing any wins or podiums, he demonstrated reliability and a solid understanding of car development. His F1 career concluded at the end of the 1995 season, but he later found success in sports car racing.
