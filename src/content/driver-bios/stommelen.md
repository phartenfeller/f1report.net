Rolf Stommelen was a versatile German racer whose career spanned Formula 1, sports cars, and touring car racing. Known for his resilience, he competed in {{driver_races}} Formula 1 Grands Prix, often in privateer entries, achieving a best finish of third at the 1970 Austrian Grand Prix. While his F1 career didn't yield wins, Stommelen found significant success in endurance racing, notably winning the Daytona 24 Hours four times. His career was marked by incredible determination, overcoming serious accidents to continue competing at the highest levels of motorsport.
