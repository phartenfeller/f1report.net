Hans Herrmann, often referred to as "Hans im Glück" (Lucky Hans), was a versatile German racing driver whose career spanned sports cars and Formula 1. Known for his remarkable ability to escape serious injury from spectacular crashes, Herrmann competed in {{driver_races}} Grand Prix races between 1953 and 1961 for marques like Mercedes-Benz, Cooper, and BRM. His best F1 result was a third-place finish at the 1954 Swiss Grand Prix. Beyond Formula 1, he achieved significant success in endurance racing, notably winning the 1970 24 Hours of Le Mans for Porsche.
