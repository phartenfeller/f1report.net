Bob Parsons was an American driver whose brief Formula 1 career comprised a single entry at the 1959 United States Grand Prix. Driving a privately entered Lotus 16, Parsons failed to qualify for the race. While his Formula 1 presence was minimal, he was a regular competitor in various American racing series during the 1950s and 1960s, often at Indianapolis. His F1 entry represents a common story of the era, where American drivers occasionally attempted the pinnacle of motorsport without extensive European experience.
