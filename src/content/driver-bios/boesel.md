Ricardo Boesel, a versatile Brazilian racer, entered 30 Formula 1 Grands Prix between 1982 and 1983. Primarily competing with March and Ligier, his F1 career was brief and yielded no points, with a best finish of seventh at the 1982 Belgian Grand Prix. Despite his limited impact in Formula 1, Boesel later achieved considerable success in sports car racing and IndyCar, including a podium finish at the Indianapolis 500 and a championship in the IMSA GTP series. His post-F1 career showcased a talent that perhaps wasn't fully realized in the pinnacle of motorsport.
