Nicola Larini, a versatile Italian driver known for his touring car successes, also carved out a niche in Formula 1 with a reputation for tenacity. Competing across several seasons, he participated in {{driver_races}} Grands Prix, notably securing a memorable second-place finish at the 1994 San Marino Grand Prix for Ferrari, deputising for the injured Jean Alesi. This podium, his sole F1 top-three result, highlighted his raw speed and determination. Despite limited opportunities in top-tier F1 machinery, Larini remained a respected figure in motorsport, primarily due to his formidable achievements outside of Grand Prix racing.
