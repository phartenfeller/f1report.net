Ayrton Senna da Silva was a Brazilian racing driver widely regarded as one of the greatest in the history of Formula 1. Renowned for his aggressive yet precise driving style, especially in wet conditions, he captivated audiences with his raw speed and intense rivalry with Alain Prost. Senna competed in {{driver_races}} Grand Prix races, securing {{driver_poles}} pole positions, {{driver_wins}} victories, and {{driver_podiums}} podium finishes. His three World Championship titles in 1988, 1990, and 1991 cemented his legacy as a fierce competitor and a legend of the sport. His tragic death at the 1994 San Marino Grand Prix left an indelible mark on Formula 1.
