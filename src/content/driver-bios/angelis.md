Elio de Angelis, the aristocratic Roman, brought a rare blend of musical talent and natural driving ability to Formula 1. Known for his smooth style and personable nature, he competed in {{driver_races}} Grands Prix, securing {{driver_wins}} victories and {{driver_podiums}} podium finishes. His career was most notably defined by his long tenure with Lotus, where he inherited the mantle of team leader after the death of Colin Chapman, and his poignant final season with Brabham in 1986. De Angelis remains a respected figure, remembered as much for his character and passion outside the car as for his competitive spirit within it.
