Luigi Serafini, often known as "Gigi," was an Italian driver whose brief Formula 1 career in the early 1950s saw him compete in a handful of Grand Prix races. Though he never secured a podium finish, Serafini notably participated in the inaugural Formula 1 World Championship race at Silverstone in 1950. His appearances, primarily with Ferrari, were limited, and he is remembered more as a participant in the sport's nascent years rather than for significant competitive achievements.
