Felice Bonetto, an Italian veteran, competed in the early years of the Formula 1 World Championship, primarily for Alfa Romeo and Maserati. Known for his robust driving style and amiable personality, Bonetto achieved {{driver_podiums}} podium finishes in his {{driver_races}} starts. He tragically died during the 1953 Carrera Panamericana, leading the race for Lancia, a testament to his continued competitive spirit beyond Grand Prix racing. His career spanned a dynamic period of motorsport, showcasing the bravery and talent required in the nascent days of F1.
