Graham Hill, an icon of the 1960s, embodied the quintessential British racing driver: debonair, witty, and fiercely competitive. A two-time World Champion, Hill competed in {{driver_races}} Grands Prix, securing {{driver_wins}} victories and {{driver_podiums}} podium finishes, often battling intense rivalries. He remains the only driver to achieve the 'Triple Crown of Motorsport', winning the Monaco Grand Prix, the Indianapolis 500, and the 24 Hours of Le Mans. His career, predominantly with BRM and Lotus, cemented his legacy as one of the sport's all-time greats.
