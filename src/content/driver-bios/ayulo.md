Frank Ayulo, often known as "Ayulo," was an Argentine driver who participated in a single Formula 1 World Championship race, the 1954 Argentine Grand Prix. A privateer entry, he drove a Maserati 250F, a car renowned for its competitive spirit in that era. Despite his brief F1 career, Ayulo was a prominent figure in South American racing during the 1950s, competing in various national and international events. His sole F1 appearance, though ending in retirement, marks him as one of the many enthusiastic privateers who contributed to the early diverse grids of the sport.
