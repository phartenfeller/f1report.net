Aguri Suzuki became a national hero in Japan, particularly for his emotional third-place finish at his home Grand Prix in 1990, driving for Larrousse. This podium, the first for a Japanese driver at the event, cemented his place in the sport's history. Known for his aggressive yet controlled driving style, Suzuki competed in {{driver_races}} Grands Prix, earning {{driver_podiums}} podiums. After his driving career, he continued his involvement in Formula 1, notably as the team principal for the Super Aguri F1 team.
