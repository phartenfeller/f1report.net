Mika Salo, a Finnish driver known for his robust, often aggressive style, competed in {{driver_races}} Formula 1 Grands Prix. While he never secured a victory, his career is most famously remembered for his stint with Ferrari in 1999, deputizing for an injured Michael Schumacher. During this period, Salo sportingly ceded a near-certain victory at the German Grand Prix to teammate Eddie Irvine, a selfless act that significantly aided Ferrari's championship aspirations. He achieved {{driver_podiums}} podium finishes across his career, demonstrating his talent and reliability even without a top-tier permanent drive.
